# SaaS已死，AaaS永生

## The SaaSpocalypse (Feb 5, 2026)

- Jefferies trader Jeffrey Favuzza coined "SaaSpocalypse"
- $285B wiped from global software stocks in 48 hours
- iShares Software ETF (IGV): $117 → $82 (-23% YTD)
- Trigger: Anthropic released 11 Claude Cowork open-source plugins for real enterprise work (contract review, compliance, sales prep, legal intake, internal research)

### Casualties
- Thomson Reuters: -16%
- London Stock Exchange Group: -13%
- Salesforce: -40% over 12 months
- ServiceNow: -28% YTD (even with good earnings)
- Oracle: -19%
- HubSpot: -20%

## US SaaS: Falling From the Cloud

- 2024 US SaaS market: $187.2B (70% global share)
- Salesforce CEO Benioff admitted cutting support from 9,000 to 5,000 ("I need less heads") — Agentforce bots replaced human agents
- A Base44 customer terminated $350K/year Salesforce contract for AI-driven custom solution
- Capital flowing from application software → AI infrastructure ($600B capex wave)

## China SaaS: Never Really Took Off

- 99% of Chinese SaaS companies are unprofitable
- Yonyou: losses of ¥1.72-1.92B (2024), widening
- Kingdee: multi-year losses, ¥142M loss (2024)
- Youzan: first profit in 2025 H1, but only via massive layoffs

### 4 Structural Barriers in China
1. **Labor cost**: cheap labor → "hire people" not "buy software"
2. **Enterprise structure**: ~4,000 large enterprises (vs ~10,000 in US), many are SOEs with different procurement. SMBs are numerous but short-lived with weak spending power
3. **Industry structure**: manufacturing/wholesale dominates, thin margins, low digitization awareness
4. **Competition**: SMBs prefer freelance devs, OEM white-label systems, GitHub forks. Standard SaaS can't compete

China SaaS market 2024: $14.5B — less than 8% of US. Top 10 companies control only 35.6%.

## AaaS: Agent as a Service

**Old SaaS logic:** Hire people → People learn software → Software improves efficiency → Save labor cost (software leverage replaces labor)

**New AaaS logic:** Deploy Agent → Agent runs autonomously → Humans review and guide → Massively reduce coordination friction (AI leverage replaces BOTH software and labor)

### Market Projections
- Markets and Markets: $7.84B (2025) → $52.62B (2030), 46.3% CAGR
- Grand View Research: $50.31B by 2030, 45.8% CAGR
- Gartner: 40% of enterprise apps will include Agentic AI by 2026 (vs <5% in 2025)
- By 2035: Agentic AI could drive 30%+ of enterprise software revenue (>$450B)
- G2 survey (Aug 2025): 57% of enterprises already running AI agents in production

- Lowest automation, human-initiated, human-edited
- Easiest for existing SaaS to implement
