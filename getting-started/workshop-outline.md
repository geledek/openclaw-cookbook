# OpenClaw Workshop — Singapore (Beginners)

*Format: 2 hours total — 1hr sharing + 1hr hands-on*
*Audience: beginners, no prior OpenClaw experience*
*Updated: 2026-02-28*

---

## Hour 1: Sharing (60 min)

### Part 1 — What is OpenClaw and is it for you? (15 min)
- The "24/7 AI employee" framing: proactive vs reactive AI
- Claws as a new layer above ChatGPT/Claude — you give it access, it acts
- Honest reality check: who's ready vs who should wait (lawrencew_zen framing — sets expectations, builds trust)
- Ray's live demo: show Bob doing something real

### Part 2 — How it works (20 min)
- 3 things OpenClaw needs: a model, a channel (Telegram/WhatsApp), and skills
- Skills = markdown folders of instructions — simpler than it sounds (Gabriel Chua)
- Memory: why agents forget and how to fix it
- The 3-phase journey beginners go through: setup → first workflow → autonomous ops (milesdeutscher)

### Part 3 — Real use cases (25 min)
- Pick 5 from the 13 verified use cases (wlzh) most relevant to this audience
  - Suggested: Reddit digest, email cleanup, personal CRM, YouTube daily, knowledge base (RAG)
- Case study: Eddie the agent — $73k/month, replaced $30k/mo agency (ernestosoftware)
- Q&A — what can it do for *your* specific situation?

---

## Hour 2: Hands-On (60 min)

### Setup (20 min)
- Deploy on Railway (cloud, no hardware needed) — fastest path for beginners
- Connect Telegram bot
- Verify it's live with a simple message

### First workflow (30 min)
- Choose one: daily digest OR email summary OR save-a-link to library
- Walk through: what the skill looks like, how to configure, how to test
- Workshop assistant agent on standby for Q&A during this phase

### Wrap-up (10 min)
- What to do next (the 3-phase roadmap)
- Community resources
- Ray's contact + follow-up channel
