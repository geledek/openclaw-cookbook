# 新手劝退 OpenClaw：99% 的人根本不需要它

> 劳伦斯's contrarian take — a useful counter-perspective for understanding who OpenClaw is actually for.

## Core Argument

99% of people don't need automation. Not because automation is bad — but because they haven't reached the stage where they need it.

Many people buy a Mac mini or spin up a VPS just to run OpenClaw, spending money without doing any real work. They saw a big-name creator posting "built a one-person company with OpenClaw, saved millions, made X dollars" — and believed it.

**The reality check:**

> You can't figure out a basic chatbot, but want multi-agent collaboration?
> You can't write a good prompt, but want AI to make autonomous decisions?
> You don't have a workflow, but want workflow automation?
> You've never touched a local agent, but want a "virtual company"?

OpenClaw is the classic product of a "skip-ahead mentality."

## The Token Waste Problem

Someone builds a virtual company: CEO, product manager, engineer, QA, finance — yes, even a finance agent. Running on your computer. An AI "finance role" — what is it actually managing? Counting how many tokens you've burned? Because it should be the first one screaming "stop."

Because OpenClaw burns through your tokens fast. Several agents "meeting," "discussing," "approving" — tokens disappear like burning money. Spend a few dollars, maybe $10+, get a half-finished result.

## The Setup Reality

Installation is simple enough. But getting it running — problems everywhere. Config conflicts, call timeouts, unpredictable results. You spend half a day debugging and realize it would've been faster to just open Claude and ask directly.

## The Transition Product Thesis

> "This is a transitional product."

Claude Code has remote control now. The big AI companies will keep shipping better, more accessible solutions. Like an "ultra-premium lobster" — served ready to eat, no need to dive into the sea yourself.

"You're currently diving into the sea to catch lobster yourself. By the time you finally get it, the table next to you already has a prepared one."

## What To Do Instead

Rather than burning tokens on this, do one meaningful thing: **Let AI into your daily work first.**

---

## Counter-perspective (for workshop balance)

This is a useful voice to include in workshop framing — helps set realistic expectations. The people who *do* benefit from OpenClaw:
- Already have working workflows they want to automate
- Have mastered single-agent prompting first
- Have a clear use case, not just FOMO
- Are comfortable debugging infrastructure issues
